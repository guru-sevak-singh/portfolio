TheOpenTable

Smart Restaurant & Theatre Food Ordering Platform

Overview

TheOpenTable is a web application designed to simplify restaurant and theatre order management. It provides an end-to-end solution for taking online and in-seat orders, monitoring sales, and tracking delivery status. Customers can place orders directly from their seats using QR codes or even opt for home delivery. On the other side, restaurant staff get a clean dashboard to manage tables, monitor payment status, and track order progress in real-time.

Key Features

  • ✅ Multi-bank account support – each theatre or outlet can connect its own bank account rather than sharing a single payment gateway.
  • ✅ Order tracking – real-time updates for pending, in-progress, and completed orders.
  • ✅ QR-based ordering – customers can scan a QR at their seat/table to order food or request home delivery.
  • ✅ Table status management – see whether a table is vacant, awaiting delivery, or has pending payments at a glance.
  • ✅ Simple dashboard – intuitive layout for staff to view all tables, orders, and payments clearly.
  • ✅ Order profiles – track individual orders with details about items, amount, and customer.
  • ✅ Payment tracking – see all payments received and pending in one place.
  • ✅ Delivery module – customers provide their details (name, address, phone) for home delivery orders, ensuring smooth communication.

Restaurant Management System Project

The Restaurant Management System was an innovative project designed to streamline operations and improve efficiency. It had a strong concept and promising features that could benefit restaurants of all sizes.

Despite its potential, the project faced tough competition from established players in the market. Most local users were already accustomed to existing products, which made adoption slow.

After giving it time and effort, we decided to conclude the project. The experience, however, provided valuable lessons in market dynamics, product positioning, and user preferences. The project’s website has now been closed, but the insights gained will guide our future endeavors.
