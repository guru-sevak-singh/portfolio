School Management System

Digital Platform for Managing Students, Teachers, and School Operations

Overview

The School Management System is an all-in-one web application designed to simplify academic and administrative workflows for schools. It enables teachers, staff, and administrators to manage students' records, track attendance, record marks, and store important documents. With dedicated dashboards and real-time reporting, it improves transparency, saves time, and reduces paperwork.

Key Features

  • ✅ Student Profiles – each student has a digital profile with personal data, attendance, marks, and documents.
  • ✅ Attendance Tracking – easy record keeping of daily attendance with automated reporting.
  • ✅ Marks & Exams – enter marks, generate performance reports, and track student progress over time.
  • ✅ Document Upload – store student-related documents (certificates, ID proofs, forms) securely.
  • ✅ Teacher Management – assign teachers to classes, monitor workloads, and manage schedules.
  • ✅ Fees & Payments – manage student fee records, track pending payments, and generate receipts.
  • ✅ Classroom Dashboard – simple overview of students, teachers, and academic activity per class.
  • ✅ Reports & Analytics – generate summary reports for student performance, attendance trends, and finances.

Smart Timetable Management

The system makes it easy to create and manage class timetables. It automatically checks teacher availability and assigns classes to free teachers, ensuring there are no conflicts and saving time in manual scheduling.

Class & Year-wise Subject Details

The system allows schools to track subject details according to each class and academic year. If a student continues in the same school for multiple years, all subject information across their entire academic journey can be accessed with a single click, directly from the student’s profile page. This ensures complete academic history management in one place.

School Management System Project

Our School Management System was successfully developed and capable of running smoothly. Working on this project helped us gain valuable insights into large databases and complex mapping.

However, despite its functionality, the project had to be stopped due to low adoption. Most users preferred traditional paper-based methods and manual boards over an online portal. Conflicts and differing priorities also made it difficult to continue the project long-term.

The system once ran successfully and demonstrated our capability to handle large-scale educational software projects.
